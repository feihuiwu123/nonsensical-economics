# Nonsensical Economics

A collection of documents analyzing counterintuitive phenomena in financial markets, focusing on cryptocurrency and stock markets.

## Overview

This repository documents persistent "nonsensical" or counterintuitive structures in financial markets—patterns that seem illogical yet continue to exist due to underlying incentives, information asymmetries, and behavioral biases. The analysis follows a consistent framework that identifies:

- **The counterintuitive phenomenon**
- **Who benefits**
- **Who loses**
- **The tools/mechanisms used to profit**
- **Why the structure persists despite its apparent irrationality**

## Content Examples

### Cryptocurrency Market (28 cases)

The cryptocurrency analysis covers 28 counterintuitive structures across three layers:

1. **Bitcoin Layer (8 cases)**
   - No cash flow, yet trillion-dollar valuation
   - Extreme volatility called a "store of value"
   - Unsuitable for payments, yet called "currency"
   - Halving as public information still priced as positive news
   - Massive energy consumption seen as valuable
   - Network congestion interpreted as "prosperity"
   - Lost private keys benefiting remaining holders
   - Centralization despite decentralization narrative

2. **Infrastructure Layer (8 cases)**
   - Decentralized assets priced on centralized exchanges
   - Stablecoins as dollar base without banking protection
   - "Stablecoins" most vulnerable to instability
   - On-chain transparency enabling front-running
   - Self-custody theoretically safest, practically riskiest
   - Derivatives (perpetuals) as main profit center vs. spot
   - Correct direction still losing due to leverage
   - ETFs bringing legitimacy and centralization

3. **Altcoin/DeFi/Platform Token Layer (12 cases)**
   - Governance tokens with voting rights but no profit rights
   - Popular products not translating to token appreciation
   - High APY as subsidy rather than real yield
   - Airdrops captured by sybil farms
   - Low float, high FDV leading to future dilution
   - Major exchange listings as exit opportunities
   - Meme coins outperforming "serious" projects
   - "Code is law" overridden in crises
   - Cross-chain bridges as vulnerability points
   - Staking yields as inflation transfer
   - KOLs profiting from referrals rather than trading
   - High-profile backings still failing

### Stock Market (40+ cases)

The stock market analysis identifies over 40 counterintuitive phenomena across five categories:

1. **Companies, Financing & Shareholder Relations**
2. **Institutions, Rules & Market Structure**
3. **Information, Intermediaries & Professional Narratives**
4. **Valuation, Themes & Capital Preferences**
5. **Psychological Biases & Behavioral Patterns**

Each case examines the structural incentives and persistent mismatches between stated purposes and actual outcomes.
